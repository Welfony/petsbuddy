The Important Role of Taurine in Cats Food

22 Dec 2018 0 comments

Amino acids are the "building blocks" of proteins. All animals require amino acids to maintain a healthy physiological function. Proteins are made up of groups of amino acids, around 10 of which cannot be produced by a cat's body and need to be supplied within the diet.

Taurine is an amino acid which can only be found in animal protein. It is not present in vegetarian protein sources like grains. It is important to maintain of a healthy heart, healthy skin and hair and good eyesight.

Previous studies have reported that taurine deficiency may arise from grain and rice-based diets where the primary protein is vegetable meals. It is hypothesized that consumption of such diets with inadequate methionine and cysteine can result in taurine deficiency in pet's body.

It is sensible for cats to consume diets with sulphur amino acid precursors of taurine, cysteine and methionine are consistent with latest animal nutritional research.
